Division Considered Harmful

When one thinks of division, they think of long division by hand. When a computer scientist thinks of division, they think of fast binary division performed by a CPU. There is another division, division over numbers in an arbitrary precision arithmetic system.

Arbitrary precision arithmetic is the next step up from fast binary arithmetic operators on machine words. Subroutines that perform this arithmetic combine multiple (or an arbitrary amount of) machine words to contain a numerical value which may be larger than what can be held in a single word. The manner in which a CPU performs arithmetic on arbitrary precision numbers is oddly similar to how a person does figures on paper. It simply iterates over all the individual words comprising the whole numerical unit.

It turns out that the algorithmic time complexity of all basic arithmetic over arbitrary precision numbers follows the time complexity of arithmetic done by hand. The state-of-the-art research for arithmetic over arbitrarily large numbers has produced only marginal improvements over the performance of naive versions of each operation.
